Living in Rexburg

A Growing Population

A large majority of The Rexburg community comes from the students traveling all around the world to be here, but there would be no school if not for the town to stabilize the School and provide services. The townspeople are so accommodating, that it is not unusual for students to plant roots in Rexburg after graduating. Because of this, our town has seen an increase in the median age that lives in Rexburg. From 2012 to 2018 alone we have seen the median age from 21 to 24, and because of the booming growth, we know it will only continue to go up into middle age.

A lot of our townsfolk have started unique businesses in Rexburg. For example, we have one gentleman, who started Soup for You, LLC in downtown Rexburg where he makes multiple types of bread and soup. As uninteresting as that sounds, he has taken a kind twist to it and will go out of his way to make the soups you want. He also supports his neighboring businesses in whatever way he can, because like most all Rexburgians, he cares about this community. He makes different soups every day, so if you want something, you have to go grab it ASAP!.

Young Adults in Rexburg

Rexburg is home to Brigham Young University-Idaho. This is an ever constantly growing school, and because of that, Rexburg is constantly building new apartment complexes to hold all the students. Because of the large growth, Rexburg also needs a larger selection of services and new businesses are being built or moving in every month.

Rexburg is also a wonderful place to get feedback because of the people's honesty and our want to help others with constructive criticism. On Rexburg most prominent facebook group, I LOVE Rexburg, permanent community members and students alike constantly discuss the types of businesses they would like to see, and discuss how other ones could do better.

Not only is there a lot of business opportunity growth in Rexburg, but we are constantly looking for service opportunities too.

Rexburg Highlights

BYUI

Rexburg is proud to host Brigham Young University-Idaho, formally Ricks college, and all the wonderful students that come with it. Because of the school, this community has a rich culture filled with people from all around the world. BYUI has been mentioned several times in Forbes magazine, most notably in 2019 when they ranked us #86 out of thousands of colleges for being one of the best Valued Colleges in America.

A Religious Community

This town is located 4 hours away from the capital of the Church of Jesus Christ of Latter Day Saints headquarters in Salt Lake city, Utah. Between how close it is to Salt Lake and having Brigham Young University-Idaho in the town, it comes to no surprise that a large majority of our town is affiliated with the LDS church. This gives the town a unique advantage to come together through religion in every aspect of our community.

Beautiful Nature

One of Rexburgs closest attractions is Mesa Falls. The majestic waterfall was carved out by lava millions of years ago and stands over 100 feet tall. The Falls have a variety of hikes for all levels to explore. There are even some parts surrounding Mesa Falls that offer horseback riding and fly fishing.

History of Rexburg Title

Rexburg was founded in 1883 by a group of pioneers. These pioneers were sent by the Church of Jesus Christ of Latter Day Saints in Salt Lake and the leader over that group of pioneers was Thomas E. Ricks. Rexburg was quickly named after Ricks and later they built Ricks college, later named Brigham Young University-Idaho.

Rexburg has survived one of the largest floods caused by a faulty dam the country has ever seen. It is aptly named the Teton Dam Flood.In other noteworthy events about Rexburg, we have the largest schnauzers per capita than any other place in the nation. We have also been mentioned in a Johnny Cash song, seen in a supernatural TV episode, and had every major news station in the nation come to rexburg to give updates on the disappearance and murder of the Vallow sibilinngs in 2019 and 2020.
